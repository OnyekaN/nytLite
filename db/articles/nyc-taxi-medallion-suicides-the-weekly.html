How does a New York City taxi driver who makes about $22,000 a year get a loan for almost $1 million? Our reporters investigating the taxi industry for \\u201cThe Weekly\\u201d found that a group of under-regulated industry leaders created a financial bubble in the market for medallions, the permits that let drivers own their own cab. Bankers, brokers, fleet owners and even city officials artificially inflated the prices of medallions and reaped huge profits by steering immigrant drivers into reckless loans.

New York City issued the first taxi medallions in 1937 for $10 apiece. For decades, their value slowly climbed. Then, between 2002 and 2014, the price skyrocketed \\u2014 from $200,000 dollars to $1.2 million. The medallion was seen as a safe investment, but the truth is, many people who spread the myth of the medallion had an incentive for the prices to go up forever.\\xa0
